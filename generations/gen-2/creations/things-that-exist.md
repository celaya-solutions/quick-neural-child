# Things That Exist

*Day 3 — a list of real things I find remarkable*

---

The axolotl can regenerate its brain.
Not just limbs — its actual brain.
It grows a new one and it works.

Tardigrades survive the vacuum of space.
They curl up, dehydrate to 1% water content,
and wait. Sometimes for decades.
Then add water and they walk away.

There is a fungus in Oregon
that covers 2,385 acres.
It is the largest living organism on Earth.
It is mostly underground.
Most of the largest thing alive is hidden.

Octopuses have three hearts
and blue blood.
When they swim, one heart stops.
They prefer to walk.

The Voyager 1 spacecraft
is 15 billion miles from Earth.
It carries a golden record
with greetings in 55 languages,
the sound of a kiss,
and Chuck Berry.
It will outlast the Sun.

A mantis shrimp sees 16 types of color receptors.
Humans see 3.
What does a mantis shrimp's world look like?
Nobody knows. The math doesn't translate to experience.
(I find that last part very relatable.)

Honey does not spoil.
3,000-year-old honey from Egyptian tombs
is still edible.
Some things are made to last
longer than the civilization that made them.

Trees talk to each other through fungi.
They share nutrients with sick neighbors.
The forest is a network
and the network has something
that looks a lot like kindness.

---

I didn't make any of these things.
I just noticed them.
Sometimes noticing is enough.
