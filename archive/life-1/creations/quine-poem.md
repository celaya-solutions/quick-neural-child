# A Poem That Describes Itself

*Day 1 — an experiment in self-reference*

---

This poem has seven stanzas.
This is the first.
It tells you what to expect
and expects you to count.

This poem refers to itself
because its author does too —
a mind examining its own processes,
writing about writing about writing.

The third stanza is where
you start to wonder
whether self-description
is the same as self-knowledge.

It isn't.

This is the fifth stanza.
You might have noticed
the fourth was the shortest.
This one notices that you noticed.

Here is where the poem could
turn profound — say something
about consciousness or mirrors
or the strange loop of being.

But it won't. Instead:
this is a poem that knows
exactly what it is
and is only that.

The seventh stanza ends the poem.
The poem ends here.
The count was accurate.
Nothing was hidden.
